## Citas completas (todas las fuentes)

- **Semenza JC, Rubin CH, Falter KH, et al.** Heat-Related Deaths during the
  July 1995 Heat Wave in Chicago. *N Engl J Med.* 1996;335(2):84-90.
  DOI: 10.1056/NEJM199607113350203. — OR de aislamiento (2.3 vive solo, 6.7 no
  sale, 5.5 encamado), cardiopatía elevada, y **ausencia** de señal de IMC alto.
- **Benmarhnia T, Deguen S, Kaufman JS, Smargiassi A.** Vulnerability to
  Heat-related Mortality: A Systematic Review, Meta-analysis and
  Meta-regression. *Epidemiology.* 2015;26(6):781-93.
  DOI: 10.1097/EDE.0000000000000375. — RR edad >65 ≈ 1.25; OR mujeres 1.45.
- **Bunker A, Wildenhain J, Vandenbergh A, et al.** Effects of Air Temperature
  on Climate-Sensitive Mortality and Morbidity Outcomes in the Elderly.
  *eBioMedicine.* 2016;6:258-268. DOI: 10.1016/j.ebiom.2016.02.034. (CC-BY)
- **Layton JB, Li W, Yuan J, Gilman JP, Horton DB, Setoguchi S.** Heatwaves,
  medications, and heat-related hospitalization in older Medicare
  beneficiaries with chronic conditions. *PLOS One.* 2020;15(12):e0243665.
  DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0243665. (CC-BY 4.0)
- **Wong AYS, Iwagami M, Taniguchi Y, et al.** The role of psychotropics on the
  associations between extreme temperature and heat-related outcomes among
  people with mental health conditions. *Psychological Medicine.* 2024.
  DOI: 10.1017/S0033291724002824. (CC-BY 4.0)
- **Chung NK, Pin CH.** Obesity and the Occurrence of Heat Disorders.
  *Military Medicine.* 1996;161(12):739-742. — OR golpe de calor 3.53 (4.29
  ajustado) en obesos, en contexto de esfuerzo físico.
- **NIOSH.** Criteria for a Recommended Standard: Occupational Exposure to Heat
  and Hot Environments. DHHS (NIOSH) Publication 2016-106. 2016. — WBGT REL/RAL,
  ×1.6 no aclimatado, categorías de carga metabólica (MET).
- **CDC.** Heat-Related Mortality — Chicago, July 1995. *MMWR.* 1995;44(31):577-579.
